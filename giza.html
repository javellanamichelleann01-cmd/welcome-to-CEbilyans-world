THE GREAT PYRAMID OF GIZA

A Masterclass in Precision & Geotechnical Engineering

Constructed over 4,500 years ago, the Great Pyramid is the only surviving wonder of the ancient world. It represents the pinnacle of ancient structural engineering, composed of over 2.3 million blocks. For modern engineers, it serves as a primary case study in logistics and high-precision measurement.

Technical Challenges & Innovation

  • The Leveling Mystery: Achieved a foundation level within 15 millimeters across 13 acres using ingenious fluid-leveling techniques.
  • Geotechnical Stability: Built on a natural bedrock outcrop to optimize bearing capacity and prevent structural settlement.
  • Astronomical Alignment: Aligned to true north within three-sixtieths of a degree, showcasing advanced early geomatics.
The Great Pyramid proves that with precise surveying and a deep understanding of your materials, you can build structures that defy time itself.
