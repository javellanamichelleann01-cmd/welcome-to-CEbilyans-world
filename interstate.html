THE INTERSTATE HIGHWAY SYSTEM

The Largest Public Works Project in Human History

Launched in 1956, this system revolutionized national mobility. Spanning over 48,000 miles, it serves as the circulatory system of the United States, allowing for high-speed travel and efficient freight movement across the continent.

Technical Challenges & Innovation

  • Structural Pavement Design: A precision multilayer system designed to support a gross vehicle weight of 80,000 lbs.
  • Standardized Safety Geometry: Implementation of strict 12-foot lane widths and 10-foot right shoulders to ensure safety at high speeds.
  • Grade Separation: Thousands of overpasses and interchanges were designed to eliminate intersections and traffic lights entirely.
