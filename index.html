OVERVIEW

Civil Engineering is a professional engineering discipline that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, including public works such as roads, bridges, canals, dams, airports, sewage systems, pipelines, structural components of buildings, and railways (American Society of Civil Engineers [ASCE], n.d.).

This portal serves as a comprehensive roadmap for the Sibilyans (the name of the Civil Engineering students at Western Mindanao State University) community and incoming freshmen at Western Mindanao State University. The name CEB1LYANS is a combination of the acronym for Civil Engineering ("CE"), the identity of the WMSU engineering students ("SIBILYANS"), and the number "1", which represents the incoming 1st-year students of the Department.

This was created to bridge the gap between academic theory and real-world application, providing a centralized resource to explore the specialized branches of Civil Engineering. Our primary goal is to foster early technical literacy and professional identity among first-year students. By outlining the core pillars of the field and showcasing global engineering milestones, this page's purpose is to inspire the next generation of engineers to build with excellence, ensuring they understand the massive societal impact of the infrastructure they will one day design and manage.

Reference: American Society of Civil Engineers. (n.d.). What is Civil Engineering? ASCE. https://www.asce.org/about-civil-engineering/

CORE COMPETENCIES

This serves as your "conceptual skeleton"

STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS

The prediction of the performance of a given structure under specified loads and effects, ensuring stability against environmental forces (Hibbeler, 2017).

MATERIAL SCIENCE

Investigating the relationship between the structure of materials and their properties to optimize infrastructure durability (Callister & Rethwisch, 2018).

GEOMATICS & SURVEYING

The science of determining the relative positions of points on or near the Earth's surface through precise measurements (Ghilani & Wolf, 2017).

HYDRAULICS & FLUIDS

Dealing with the mechanical properties of liquids and the application of fluid mechanics to engineering problems (Mott & Untener, 2016).

SPECIALIZATIONS

Get to know each specializations and be familiar with some of it's works.

Structural Engineering

The study and design of structures that must support or resist loads. It ensures that buildings, bridges, and towers remain stable and secure against forces such as gravity, wind, and seismic activity (American Society of Civil Engineers [ASCE], n.d.).

Burj Khalifa: Record-breaking height core.
Akashi Kaikyō Bridge: Longest suspension span.
The Pantheon: Unreinforced concrete ancient wonder.
Branch Source: American Society of Civil Engineers. (n.d.). Structural engineering. ASCE. Marvel Source: ASCE. (2021). Seven wonders of the modern world. American Society of Civil Engineers.
Construction & Management Engineering

The branch responsible for the physical execution of a project. It involves the planning and management of construction operations, focusing on budget, scheduling, and the coordination of onsite materials and labor (Peurifoy et al., 2018).

Great Wall of China: Miraculous ancient logistics.
Empire State Building: Built in record 410 days.
Eurotunnel: Massive underwater TBM coordination.
Branch Source: Peurifoy, R. L., Schexnayder, C. J., Vazquez, J. P., & Shapira, A. (2018). Construction planning, equipment, and methods. McGraw-Hill Education. Marvel Source: National Geographic Society. (2022). Engineering the Empire State Building. National Geographic Education.
Transportation Engineering

The application of technology and scientific principles to the planning, functional design, and management of facilities for any mode of transportation to provide for the safe, rapid, and environmentally compatible movement of people and goods (Khisty & Lall, 2017).

Roman Appian Way: The ancestor of all modern roads.
Danyang–Kunshan Bridge: World's longest bridge.
Pan-American Highway: Cross-continent connectivity.
Source: Khisty, C. J., & Lall, B. K. (2017). Transportation engineering: An introduction. Pearson. Marvel Source: Guinness World Records. (2024). Longest bridge: Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge.
Water Resources Engineering

A discipline focused on the collection and management of water as a natural resource. This includes the design of systems for irrigation, regional water supply, flood control, and the management of rivers and estuaries (Mays, 2019).

Three Gorges Dam: Hydroelectric power peak.
London Sewerage System: Victorian engineering marvel.
Hoover Dam: Thermal management pioneer.
Source: Mays, L. W. (2019). Water resources engineering. John Wiley & Sons. Marvel Source: U.S. Bureau of Reclamation. (2023). The story of Hoover Dam. Department of the Interior.
Geotechnical Engineering

This field applies the principles of soil and rock mechanics to investigate subsurface conditions. It is essential for designing foundations, slopes, and retaining structures to ensure they interact safely with the earth (Das & Sobhan, 2021).

Kansai Airport: Artificial island settlement design.
Tower of Pisa: Stabilized soil extraction.
Palm Jumeirah: Land reclamation miracle.
Source: Das, B. M., & Sobhan, K. (2021). Principles of geotechnical engineering. Cengage Learning. Marvel Source: Burland, J. B. (2001). Saving the Leaning Tower of Pisa: A Geotechnical Miracle. Ground Engineering.
Environmental and Energy Engineering

Involves the integration of environmental protection and energy resource management. It focuses on reducing the environmental footprint of energy systems and improving the sustainability of natural resource extraction and consumption (Davis & Masten, 2020).

Bhadla Solar Park: World's largest renewable farm.
Thames Barrier: London's tidal surge protection.
Itaipu Dam: Clean energy global leader.
Gansu Wind Farm: Massive-scale wind energy production.
Source: Davis, M. L., & Masten, S. J. (2020). Principles of environmental engineering & science. McGraw-Hill Education. Marvel Source: International Hydropower Association. (2023). Itaipu Binacional: Global renewable energy leadership.
